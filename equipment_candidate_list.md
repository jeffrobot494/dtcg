# Equipment Candidate List

This document lists candidate equipment for the vertical slice prototype. The assumed equipment slots are:

- **1 Ring** — weaker, tactical, narrow effects
- **1 Amulet** — stronger, build-defining effects
- **1 Staff** — most powerful, run-shaping effects

The goal is for each equipment choice to create a real strategic decision rather than a simple numerical upgrade.

---

## Rings

Rings should generally be the weakest equipment slot. They are best used for narrow passive bonuses, one-time tactical effects, or small consistency tools.

### 1. Gravebinder Ring

**Effect:** After you win a battle, choose one card in your graveyard. Shuffle it into your deck instead of losing it permanently.

**Role:** Protects one important card per fight. Very relevant to the core permanent-card-loss mechanic.

**Notes:** This may be too strong for a ring and could instead be moved to the amulet slot.

---

### 2. Ring of Redirection

**Effect:** Sacrifice this ring: change the target of target spell.

**Role:** One-time protection against burn, land destruction, removal, or a boss spell.

**Notes:** Especially useful against red mages. Creates a real choice between recurring value and a single emergency answer.

---

### 3. Ring of the Faint Pulse

**Effect:** At the start of each battle, gain 1 life. If you have 5 or less life, gain 3 life instead.

**Role:** Survival ring. Helps prevent death spirals.

**Notes:** Stronger and more interesting than simply gaining 1 life every battle because it becomes a desperation tool.

---

### 4. Bone Signet

**Effect:** Once per battle, when one of your nontoken creatures dies, create a 1/1 Skeleton token.

**Role:** Softens creature attrition without saving the original card.

**Notes:** Good for making creature combat less punishing while preserving the core card-loss tension.

---

### 5. Ring of the Miser

**Effect:** After each battle, gain 1 gold for each enemy creature that died.

**Role:** Economy item. Helps merchant-focused play.

**Notes:** Does not help win battles directly, but improves long-term preparation.

---

### 6. Ring of the Ghoul-Keeper

**Effect:** At the start of your first turn, put an **Honored Ghoul** card from your deck into your hand.

**Role:** Consistency item. Makes one specific card or deck plan more reliable.

**Notes:** Becomes interesting if Honored Ghoul can be permanently lost. The equipment gets weaker if the player loses the card it depends on.

---

### 7. Ring of Ashen Blood

**Effect:** The first time each battle you gain life, gain 2 additional life.

**Role:** Lifegain synergy ring. Good with black drain cards.

**Notes:** Weak if the player has no lifegain, strong if the deck is built around draining or recovering life.

---

## Amulets

Amulets should be stronger than rings and should push the player toward a recognizable playstyle. They can be build-defining, risky, or highly synergistic.

### 1. Amulet of the Last Grave

**Effect:** After you win a battle, choose one card in your graveyard. Shuffle it into your deck instead of losing it permanently.

**Role:** Protects one card from permanent loss after each victory.

**Notes:** This is the same effect as Gravebinder Ring, but it may belong better in the amulet slot because it directly protects the player from the main attrition mechanic.

---

### 2. Amulet of Black Flame

**Effect:** Once each turn, when you cast a spell, you may pay 2 life instead of paying one `{B}`.

**Role:** Turns life into mana. Very black, very dangerous.

**Notes:** Strong with lifegain. Risky because life persists across battles. The “once each turn” limit prevents it from becoming too explosive.

---

### 3. Amulet of the Open Vein

**Effect:** Whenever a creature enters the battlefield under your control, lose 1 life and add `{B}`.

**Role:** Explosive engine for tokens, zombies, and cheap creatures.

**Notes:** Very powerful with cards that create multiple creatures. Dangerous because it can quickly drain the player’s persistent life total.

---

### 4. Serpent Amulet

**Effect:** At the beginning of your end step, draw a card. If you control a Snake, you gain 2 life and each opponent loses 2 life. Otherwise, you lose 2 life.

**Role:** Powerful draw engine and Snake build-around.

**Notes:** Excellent if one of the legendary creatures is a Snake. Without a Snake, the amulet still draws cards but bleeds the player.

---

### 5. Amulet of the Grave-Tithe

**Effect:** Whenever one of your nontoken creatures dies, each opponent loses 1 life and you gain 1 life.

**Role:** Makes creature losses hurt less and supports sacrifice, attrition, and zombie strategies.

**Notes:** Does not prevent permanent card loss, but turns death into value.

---

### 6. Amulet of the Second Heart

**Effect:** At the start of each battle, gain 5 temporary life. This life disappears after the battle.

**Role:** Helps survive dangerous fights without permanently raising the player’s life total.

**Notes:** Good for the cave or boss fight. Safer than permanent healing because it cannot be farmed into long-term life advantage.

---

### 7. Amulet of the Necromancer’s Price

**Effect:** Whenever you would lose a card permanently after battle, you may pay 2 life to save that card instead.

**Role:** Direct life-for-card tradeoff.

**Notes:** Very thematic and strategically interesting, but potentially very strong. May need a limit such as “up to once per battle” or “up to three cards per battle.”

---

### 8. Amulet of Red Ruin

**Effect:** The first time an opponent casts a red spell each battle, that spell costs them 1 additional mana.

**Role:** Anti-red tech.

**Notes:** Very useful for this vertical slice because all enemy sorcerors are red mages, but may be less interesting in the full game unless enemy colors vary.

---

## Staffs

Staffs should be the most powerful equipment slot. They should have effects that can reshape the player’s plan for an entire run or dramatically alter battle strategy.

### 1. Staff of the Last Rite

**Effect:** Once per battle, return a card from your graveyard to your hand.

**Role:** Saves a card from permanent loss and lets you reuse it during the battle.

**Notes:** Very strong because it protects key cards and gives in-battle value. Likely one of the cleanest and strongest staff designs.

---

### 2. Staff of the Deep Crypt

**Effect:** At the start of each battle, create two 1/1 Zombie tokens.

**Role:** Gives board presence without risking real cards.

**Notes:** Very valuable in a game where dead real cards are permanently lost. Strong with sacrifice effects and creature-buff effects.

---

### 3. Staff of the Blood Host

**Effect:** Creatures you control get +1/+0. Zombies and Skeletons you control get an additional +1/+0.

**Role:** Makes the village raid and undead strategy much stronger.

**Notes:** Straightforward but useful. Gives a clear reason to pursue zombie rewards.

---

### 4. Staff of the Black Bargain

**Effect:** Sacrifice this staff: search your deck for a card, put it into your hand, then shuffle. Lose life equal to that card’s mana value.

**Role:** Emergency tutor. Very powerful, but costly and one-use.

**Notes:** Could become repetitive if the player always searches for the same best card. Consider restricting it to creatures, spells of mana value 3 or less, or non-legendary cards.

---

### 5. Staff of the False Grave

**Effect:** The first nontoken creature that dies each turn returns to the battlefield under an opponent’s control.

**Role:** Weird cursed staff. Creates chaotic necromantic tug-of-war gameplay.

**Notes:** Very flavorful, but rules-heavy. Could make games confusing or long. Probably best as a rare cursed item rather than a core prototype item.

---

### 6. Staff of the Devouring Pit

**Effect:** Whenever one of your creatures dies, add `{B}` at the start of your next main phase.

**Role:** Turns sacrifice and creature loss into mana.

**Notes:** Safer and slower than Amulet of the Open Vein. Good for attrition decks.

---

### 7. Staff of the Withering March

**Effect:** At the beginning of each battle, each opponent loses 2 life and you gain 2 life.

**Role:** Strong attrition advantage, especially against multiple boss sorcerors.

**Notes:** Very clean and powerful. Particularly relevant if the boss fight has multiple enemies.

---

### 8. Staff of Night’s Shelter

**Effect:** The first time each battle you would take damage from a spell, prevent that damage.

**Role:** Anti-burn defensive staff.

**Notes:** Very useful against red mages. Makes the player better prepared for direct-damage sorcerors without shutting them down completely.

---

### 9. Staff of the Corpse Road

**Effect:** Whenever you win a battle, if three or more creatures died under your control, add a random Zombie card to your deck.

**Role:** Lets the player grow stronger through bloody fights.

**Notes:** Good for players who embrace attrition. Rewards creature-heavy, sacrifice-heavy, or zombie-heavy strategies.

---

## Suggested Tight Vertical Slice Set

If the first prototype needs a smaller, focused selection, the following set gives a strong spread of effects:

### Rings

- **Ring of Redirection** — one-time tactical spell protection
- **Ring of the Faint Pulse** — survival and anti-death-spiral
- **Ring of the Ghoul-Keeper** — consistency around Honored Ghoul
- **Bone Signet** — softens creature attrition

### Amulets

- **Amulet of Black Flame** — life-for-mana acceleration
- **Serpent Amulet** — build-around card draw and Snake synergy
- **Amulet of the Grave-Tithe** — death/lifedrain attrition engine

### Staffs

- **Staff of the Last Rite** — graveyard rescue and card reuse
- **Staff of the Deep Crypt** — free zombie tokens each battle

---

## Design Notes

The strongest equipment effects are the ones that interact with the prototype’s core pressures:

- Permanent card loss
- Persistent life total
- Limited healing
- Enemy deck rewards
- Optional monster fights
- Crafting and material scarcity
- Boss preparation

Equipment should generally bend the attrition rules rather than cancel them. Effects that completely remove permanent card loss are likely to undermine the central premise. Better effects force tradeoffs, such as saving one card, paying life to preserve cards, gaining temporary life, creating expendable tokens, or profiting when creatures die.
